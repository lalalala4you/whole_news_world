## 🌍 Top 5 Global Stories

### #1 | Keir Starmer Resigns as UK Prime Minister, Andy Burnham Poised to Take Over

British Prime Minister Keir Starmer announced his resignation as Labour Party leader, triggering a leadership contest. Greater Manchester Mayor Andy Burnham has emerged as the frontrunner to succeed him, returning to Westminster after being sworn in as MP for Makerfield. Starmer said he will remain PM until his successor is chosen, while at least one other senior Labour figure is considering a bid to prevent a "coronation." The resignation comes amid growing political pressure following Labour's rocky term and the fallout from the Iran conflict.

**Why it matters:** This marks the first change of British leadership since July 2024 and reshapes the UK's political landscape at a critical juncture involving post-Brexit EU relations and US-UK alignment on Iran.

🏷️ UK / Politics / Geopolitics

### #2 | US-Iran Nuclear Talks Show Progress but Tensions Remain High

The US has eased oil sanctions on Iran as negotiations in Switzerland advanced, with Vice President JD Vance describing the talks as laying a "very good foundation for a successful final deal." Iran has agreed to the return of UN nuclear inspectors as part of the emerging agreement. However, the talks were strained when President Trump issued fresh threats against Tehran, prompting an Iranian walkout. Iran's foreign ministry denied making "new commitments" on nuclear inspections, while the White House maintained that progress is being made toward a comprehensive deal.

**Why it matters:** A US-Iran nuclear deal would reshape Middle Eastern geopolitics, affect global oil markets, and potentially de-escalate the broader Iran war that fractured US-UK relations under Starmer.

🏷️ Middle East / US-Iran / Nuclear Diplomacy

### #3 | China Escalates Trade War with US Over Rare Earths and Defence Firms

China has moved against US rare earth and defence companies in what it called a "calibrated" response to the Pentagon's latest blacklist of Chinese firms. The retaliatory measures target critical mineral supply chains and major US defence contractors. Separately, China arrested a US academic at a conference on espionage charges, further escalating bilateral tensions. The moves come as GlobalFoundries announced Singapore is key to its plans for equipping humanoid robots with AI, underscoring the semiconductor supply chain's central role in US-China competition.

**Why it matters:** Rare earth elements are essential for everything from EVs to military hardware — China controls roughly 60% of global production, giving this trade war direct implications for global tech and defence supply chains.

🏷️ US-China / Trade War / Technology

### #4 | At Least 13 Killed, Dozens Injured in Qatar Gas Explosion

A massive gas explosion in Qatar has killed at least 13 people and injured dozens more, according to authorities. Emergency services responded to the blast site, with rescue operations continuing through the day. The cause of the explosion is under investigation. The incident comes as Qatar plays host to World Cup 2026 matches, though officials have not indicated any link to football-related activities.

**Why it matters:** The explosion highlights industrial safety concerns in a major energy-exporting nation and adds a sombre note during the World Cup being hosted across North America with Qatar's ongoing international engagement.

🏷️ Qatar / Disaster / Middle East

### #5 | Myanmar Military Killed Over 700 Civilians in Six Months, UN Reports

A new UN report has documented that Myanmar's military junta killed over 700 civilians in the past six months, marking a significant escalation in violence since the 2021 coup. The report details systematic attacks on civilian populations, including airstrikes on villages and mass arrests. International calls for action have intensified, but the junta continues to reject external intervention amid the ongoing civil war with pro-democracy resistance forces and ethnic armed groups.

**Why it matters:** The escalating civilian death toll represents one of the world's worst ongoing humanitarian crises, with Myanmar's conflict destabilising Southeast Asia and driving refugee flows across the region.
