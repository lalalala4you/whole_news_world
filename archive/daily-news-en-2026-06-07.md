### #1 | 🏆 Iran-US War Reaches 100th Day as US Shoots Down Drones Near Strait of Hormuz

The United States shot down two Iranian drones threatening the Strait of Hormuz, marking the 100th day of the Iran-US war with no ceasefire in sight. **Why it matters:** The prolonged war is disrupting global oil shipments through the Strait of Hormuz, rattling world markets, and creating dangerous pressure on US President Trump ahead of midterm elections.

### #2 | ☢️ Russian Drone Strikes Spent Nuclear Fuel Storage at Chornobyl; Zelensky Heads to London Summit

A Russian drone hit a storage facility for spent nuclear fuel near the Chornobyl nuclear plant in northern Ukraine, partially destroying the building. Ukrainian officials said radiation levels remain stable and the fire was extinguished, but President Zelensky condemned it as a "vile" deliberate strike on nuclear infrastructure. The attack came as Zelensky prepared to meet UK PM Starmer, French President Macron, and German Chancellor Merz for talks in London on European security guarantees for Ukraine. Separately, Putin rejected Zelensky's call for face-to-face talks, insisting Russia's war goals must be met first, while Ukraine launched an "unprecedented" drone attack on St Petersburg.

**Why it matters:** The strike on a nuclear facility raises the spectre of radiological disaster and underscores the escalating nature of the conflict as US attention shifts to the Iran war.

### #3 | 🗳️ Armenia Votes in Pivotal Election: Pro-West Government vs Russian Pressure

Armenians went to the polls on June 7 in an election that could determine whether the country continues its westward shift or returns to Moscow's orbit. Incumbent PM Nikol Pashinyan faces declining domestic support (from 54% to ~30%) over his handling of the Nagorno-Karabakh loss and the divisive peace deal with Azerbaijan. Russia has stepped up economic pressure, banning exports of Armenian flowers, mineral water, cognac, and fresh produce days before the vote. Moscow is Armenia's largest trading partner, accounting for 36% of foreign trade. Pashinyan's main challenger is billionaire Samvel Karapetyan, who made his fortune in Russia and is currently under house arrest.

**Why it matters:** This election is a test case for post-Soviet nations weighing EU integration against economic dependence on Russia, with ripple effects across the Caucasus.

UK Deputy PM David Lammy told the BBC he called US Vice-President JD Vance directly to tell him his comments blaming the murder of British student Henry Novak on the "mass invasion of migrants" were "wrong" — the killer was British-born. The transatlantic relationship has been further strained by the UK's decision not to join US offensive military action in the Iran war.

**Why it matters:** The public breakdown in diplomatic norms between senior US and UK officials signals the deepest rift in the "special relationship" in decades, with far-right rhetoric on migration amplifying tensions across Europe.
