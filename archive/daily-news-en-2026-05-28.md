## 🌍 Top 5 Global Stories

### #1 | US Carries Out New Strikes on Iran, Ceasefire on the Brink

The US military launched fresh strikes on a military site in Bandar Abbas, Iran's strategic port city, while also shooting down four Iranian attack drones near the Strait of Hormuz. US Central Command described the operation as "measured, purely defensive" but Iran condemned it as "a grave violation of the ceasefire" and vowed retaliation. President Trump told his cabinet that Iran is "negotiating on fumes" and the US is "not satisfied," threatening to resume large-scale bombing if Tehran does not agree to his terms. The three-month war has already choked traffic in the crucial Strait of Hormuz and sent global energy prices soaring.

**Why it matters:** A collapse of the fragile US-Iran ceasefire would destabilize global oil markets and potentially reignite full-scale conflict in the Middle East.

🏷️ Geopolitics / Middle East War

### #2 | Israel Issues Largest Lebanon Evacuation Order Since Ceasefire

The Israeli military ordered residents across swathes of southern Lebanon — areas south of the Zahrani River covering roughly 14% of Lebanese territory — to evacuate, declaring them "combat zones" ahead of fresh strikes against Hezbollah. More than 150 Israeli airstrikes tore through some 50 towns and villages in 24 hours, killing at least 31 people including 15 in Burj al-Shamali near Tyre. Hezbollah fighters clashed with Israeli troops "at point-blank range" north of the Litani River, and Prime Minister Netanyahu said forces are "deepening" their ground operation. Iran insists any broader peace deal must also cover Lebanon.

**Why it matters:** The escalation threatens to derail US-Iran peace talks and risks a wider regional war involving Israel, Hezbollah, and Iran simultaneously.

🏷️ Middle East / Israel-Lebanon

### #3 | Nearly 500,000 Russian Soldiers Killed in Ukraine, UK Spy Chief Reveals

GCHQ director Anne Keast-Butler revealed in her inaugural public speech that almost half a million Russian soldiers have been killed since the full-scale invasion of Ukraine in 2022. She warned the UK is at a "moment of consequence" with Russia "relentlessly targeting" critical infrastructure and waging an undeclared "hybrid war" against Britain and NATO allies — including cyber attacks, sabotage, and assassination attempts. She also cautioned that China is now a "science and tech superpower" with sophisticated intelligence and cyber capabilities, and that the window for the West to stay ahead in AI is narrowing fast.

**Why it matters:** The staggering casualty figure underscores the war's devastating human toll, while the hybrid warfare warning signals a new phase of Russia-NATO confrontation beyond the battlefield.

🏷️ Ukraine War / Intelligence

### #4 | China's EV Dominance Reaches Tipping Point as Foreign Carmakers Collapse

Global carmakers are facing an existential crisis as Chinese rivals set an unrelenting pace in electric vehicles, batteries, design, and software. Foreign brands' share of China's car market has plunged from 64% in 2020 to just 32% this year, and Honda's CEO admitted after visiting a Shanghai factory: "We have no chance against this." At Xiaomi's Beijing plant, a car rolls off the line every 76 seconds; BYD has developed chargers that add 400km of range in five minutes. Chinese EVs are 30% cheaper to produce thanks to state-backed supply chains that dominate more than 315 product categories globally.

**Why it matters:** The reshaping of the $3 trillion global auto industry by Chinese EV makers represents the most significant shift in manufacturing power since Japan's rise in the 1980s.

🏷️ Economy / Technology

### #5 | IISS: Taiwan Conflict Would Risk US-China Nuclear Escalation

Ahead of Asia's biggest annual defence summit — the Shangri-La Dialogue in Singapore this weekend — the International Institute for Strategic Studies (IISS) published a stark assessment: any US-China conflict over Taiwan would risk nuclear escalation, with both militaries likely to target each other's command and communications hubs. The London-based think tank warned the world is "on the cusp of a new nuclear arms race with the Asia-Pacific at its core," as regional powers expand atomic arsenals and pursue long-range conventional strike capabilities. The report follows the Xi-Trump summit in Beijing earlier this month, which sparked concern in Taipei about US commitment to the island's defence.

**Why it matters:** The world's two largest economies and military powers lack clear "guard rails" to prevent a Taiwan conflict from spiraling into the gravest crisis since the Cold War.
