### #3 | Texas AG Ken Paxton Defeats John Cornyn in Senate Primary Upset
Texas Attorney General Ken Paxton decisively routed veteran incumbent Senator John Cornyn in the Republican Senate primary, setting up a closely watched general election contest in November. Cornyn, who served in the Senate since 2002, was targeted by Trump-aligned conservatives who viewed him as insufficiently loyal. Paxton's victory underscores the continued transformation of the Republican Party and Trump's enduring influence over GOP primary voters.

**Why it matters:** The result reshapes the battle for Senate control ahead of the 2026 midterms, with Paxton now positioned as a leading figure in the Trump wing of the party.

### #4 | 82 Killed in China's Deadliest Coal Mine Disaster in 16 Years
A gas explosion at the Liushenyu coal mine in Shanxi province has killed at least 82 people, with two still missing, making it China's worst mining disaster since 2010. On China's tightly controlled internet, anger is growing as citizens question how such an accident could happen and call for accountability. The tragedy comes amid China's renewed push for coal production to meet energy security needs, despite its public commitments to green transition.

**Why it matters:** The disaster exposes tensions between China's energy security imperatives and safety standards, fuelling rare public dissent on Chinese social media.

🏷️ China / Disaster
