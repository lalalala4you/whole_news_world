## 🌍 Top 5 Global Stories

### #1 | Magnitude 7.8 Earthquake Devastates Philippines' Mindanao Island

A powerful 7.8-magnitude earthquake struck the southern Philippines on Monday, killing at least 37 people and injuring nearly 500 others. The quake, triggered by movement on the Cotabato Trench, has left large swaths of Mindanao without electricity or telecoms, with nearly 2,000 homes and 6,000 schools damaged. Hundreds of aftershocks continue to jolt the region, and officials fear the death toll could rise as search and rescue operations reach isolated communities. Schools held Monday flag ceremonies outdoors — a practice that likely saved hundreds of students from collapsing buildings.

**Why it matters:** One of the strongest quakes to hit the Philippines in decades threatens a humanitarian crisis on an island already facing access challenges, with landslides cutting off entire towns.

### #2 | Israel and Iran Exchange Strikes as Middle East Ceasefires Fray

Over the weekend, Israel bombed sites in Iran for the first time since an April ceasefire, after Iran fired missiles at Israel in retaliation for Israeli strikes on Beirut. US President Trump told the BBC that Israeli planes were "already on their way" when he called Netanyahu to urge restraint, but denied the Israeli PM had defied him, insisting they have "full co-ordination." Both sides now signal the exchange is over, and Trump claims a peace deal — including reopening the Strait of Hormuz — could come within "two or three days." Meanwhile, Israeli strikes on the Lebanese city of Tyre have killed at least eight people.

**Why it matters:** The fragility of the US-brokered ceasefires raises questions about whether Washington can truly contain its allies, while Tehran appears emboldened and may use the moment to strengthen its negotiating position.

### #3 | Xi Jinping Visits Pyongyang in Bid to Reassert China's Influence Over North Korea

Chinese President Xi Jinping arrived in North Korea for his first official visit since 2019, aiming to revitalise ties with a strategically vital but increasingly unpredictable partner that has drawn closer to Russia. Xi received a colourful welcome in Pyongyang, with observers noting the trip is less about friendship and more about leverage as Beijing competes with Moscow for influence over Kim Jong Un's regime. Xi has since departed with what analysts describe as "renewed signs of China's influence" over the isolated state.

**Why it matters:** With North Korea deepening its military cooperation with Russia amid the Ukraine war, Xi's visit signals Beijing's determination not to lose its traditional grip on its only formal ally.

🏷️ Geopolitics / East Asia

### #4 | OpenAI Files for IPO as Three AI Giants Race to Public Markets

OpenAI, the company behind ChatGPT, has made a confidential filing with the SEC to pursue an initial public offering, following rival Anthropic's IPO filing last week. SpaceX is also preparing for a stock market debut targeting a $1.75 trillion valuation. The three firms collectively need vast capital to fund AI infrastructure, chips, and model training — OpenAI alone spends an estimated $100 billion annually on compute. All three say no timeline has been set, but the race to debut first is intensifying as private valuations approach $1 trillion each.

**Why it matters:** The IPOs will be a defining moment for the generative AI industry, testing whether public markets can sustain the enormous valuations of companies that are still burning cash at historic rates.

🏷️ Technology / Business

### #5 | Trump Booed at NBA Finals in New York as Knicks Fall to Spurs

Donald Trump became the first sitting US president to attend an NBA Finals game on Monday, appearing at Madison Square Garden for Game 3 between the New York Knicks and San Antonio Spurs. The president was met with thunderous boos when shown on the arena's jumbotron, while intense security measures gridlocked midtown Manhattan, shutting down streets for blocks and frustrating thousands of fans. The Knicks lost 115-111, narrowing their series lead to 2-1. Trump told reporters the reception was "mostly cheers" and "very enthusiastic."

**Why it matters:** The event underscores the deep political divide in Trump's hometown and the unprecedented security footprint of presidential attendance at major sporting events.
