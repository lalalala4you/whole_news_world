## 🌍 Top 5 Global Stories

### #1 | Trump Warns Iran Deal 'Not Final' Ahead of Friday Signing — 'I Am the Boss' at G7

The US-Iran peace deal that was supposed to end the months-long Gulf conflict faced its most serious test yet, as President Trump declared from the G7 summit in France that the agreement is "not final" and warned the US could "go back to dropping bombs" if Tehran doesn't comply to his satisfaction. The draft accord envisions an end to hostilities and the reopening of the Strait of Hormuz, but skeptical Republicans are demanding details before the formal signing scheduled for Friday. G7 leaders also called for a broader ceasefire in Lebanon and wider talks on Iran's missile program, while Trump asserted "I am the boss" in summit negotiations. Oil markets remain on edge.

**Why it matters:** The fate of the most consequential US-Iran diplomacy in decades — and global oil stability — hangs on Trump's Friday decision.

### #2 | Israel Launches Fresh Strikes on Lebanon as Netanyahu Defies Trump Criticism

Israel carried out new airstrikes on Lebanon despite direct criticism from President Trump, who said Prime Minister Netanyahu needed "to be more responsible with respect to Lebanon." The strikes come as Hezbollah is seen positioning to gain politically from the broader Iran-US diplomatic framework. The G7 leaders' statement explicitly called for a Lebanon ceasefire, but Netanyahu's government appears determined to press its military advantage before any regional settlement takes shape. This is the most significant US-Israel friction since the war began.

**Why it matters:** Open US-Israel rift over Lebanon tests the limits of American influence over its closest Middle East ally.

### #3 | China Arrests US Academic at Conference for 'Espionage Activities'

Chinese authorities have arrested an American academic attending a conference in China on charges of "espionage activities," marking the most serious US-China diplomatic incident in months. The arrest comes amid already strained relations over Taiwan, trade, and technology competition. The US State Department has demanded immediate consular access and warned Americans about travel to China. The case echoes previous instances of China using foreign nationals as diplomatic bargaining chips, and risks derailing tentative efforts to stabilize bilateral ties.

**Why it matters:** A flashpoint that could freeze US-China relations just as both sides were exploring de-escalation.

🏷️ US-China / Diplomacy

### #4 | Bank of Japan Raises Interest Rates to 31-Year High of 1%

The Bank of Japan raised its key interest rate to 1% — the highest level in 31 years — as the country continues its historic exit from decades of ultra-loose monetary policy. The move, which comes as Japan faces record summer temperatures and investigates ice cream price-fixing cartels, signals confidence in the economy but also reflects concern about imported inflation from a weak yen. The rate hike rippled through Asian markets and is being watched closely by global investors for signs of further normalization in the world's third-largest economy.

**Why it matters:** Japan's monetary normalization is one of the most significant shifts in global finance — ending an era of near-zero rates that defined three decades.

🏷️ Economy / Asia

### #5 | Messi Rewrites History at Record Sixth World Cup — Scores Hat-trick 20 Years After Debut

Lionel Messi, at age 38, scored a stunning hat-trick for Argentina exactly 20 years to the day after his World Cup debut, cementing his legacy at a record sixth tournament. On the same day, Kylian Mbappé broke French records against Senegal, overtaking Olivier Giroud as France's all-time top scorer, and Erling Haaland scored twice as Norway beat Iraq 4-1 on their World Cup return. England prepare to open their campaign against Croatia at the Dallas Stadium, while India — the world's most populous nation at 1.4 billion — remains absent from football's biggest stage.

**Why it matters:** The 2026 World Cup is delivering generational sporting theatre, with Messi's twilight brilliance and Mbappé's ascendance defining the tournament's narrative.
