## 🌍 Top 5 Global Stories

### #1 | Venezuela Earthquake Death Toll Surges Past 188 as Twin Quakes Devastate Caracas

The death toll from Wednesday's twin earthquakes in Venezuela has climbed to at least 188, with nearly 1,500 injured, as rescuers continue to pull survivors and bodies from the rubble. Two powerful shallow quakes — magnitude 7.2 followed seconds later by a 7.5 — struck the Yaracuy region west of Caracas and devastated parts of the capital itself, collapsing buildings, hospitals, and highways. Interim President Delcy Rodríguez declared a state of emergency and mobilised the military for search-and-rescue operations. The US Geological Survey warns the final death toll could reach 10,000 to 100,000 given the prevalence of unreinforced adobe and brick construction in densely populated areas. Video footage shows frantic residents fleeing as concrete crumbles around them, and international aid teams are beginning to mobilise. President Trump said the US is "ready to assist," while neighbouring countries have offered support.

**Why it matters:** Venezuela's deadliest earthquake in a century strikes a country already battered by political turmoil and economic collapse, compounding a humanitarian crisis and testing the interim government's capacity to respond.

🏷️ Natural Disaster / Latin America

### #2 | Supreme Court Rules Trump Can End Protected Status for Haitian & Syrian Immigrants

The US Supreme Court handed the Trump administration a significant victory on Thursday, ruling 6-3 along ideological lines that the president has broad authority to end Temporary Protected Status (TPS) for hundreds of thousands of immigrants from Haiti, Syria, and other countries. The decision opens the door for deportation proceedings against approximately 350,000 TPS holders who have lived legally in the US for years, many for over a decade. The majority opinion held that the executive branch maintains wide discretion over immigration enforcement, while the liberal dissent warned the ruling "circumvents" decades of settled immigration law. The decision comes amid a broader immigration crackdown and fierce debate ahead of the November midterm elections. Separately, the Court also ruled to scale back Roundup cancer lawsuits in a win for manufacturer Bayer.

**Why it matters:** The ruling reshapes the legal landscape for one of the largest immigrant populations in the US, with profound implications for families, communities, and the political landscape heading into election season.

🏷️ US Politics / Immigration

### #3 | Record Heatwave Scorches Europe — Germany Braces for 40°C, Paris Restricts Alcohol

Europe's record-shattering June heatwave continues to spread eastward, with Germany now bracing for temperatures that could hit 40°C in some areas. France, which logged its hottest day ever earlier this week, has imposed restrictions on alcohol consumption and sales in Paris to ease pressure on hospitals overwhelmed by heat-related admissions. Red heat alerts remain active across France, the UK, Italy, Spain, and now Germany, Poland, Croatia, and Hungary. Several Parisian landmarks including the Louvre and Eiffel Tower have closed early due to buildings "not sufficiently adapted to climate change." Wildfires have erupted in western France, and drowning deaths have been reported in Germany as people seek relief in rivers. Spain recorded its highest daily average June temperatures since 1950 on consecutive days earlier this week.

**Why it matters:** Europe is warming at twice the global average rate, making these events a stark preview of what climate change means for infrastructure, public health, and daily life across the continent.

🏷️ Climate / Europe

### #4 | UN Pauses Strait of Hormuz Evacuation Plan After Cargo Ship Attacked

The United Nations has paused its Strait of Hormuz evacuation and de-escalation plan after a cargo ship was struck by an "unknown projectile" near Oman. No casualties were reported, but the incident has raised fresh concerns about maritime security in the vital waterway through which roughly 20% of the world's oil passes. The attack comes amid a fragile ceasefire between the US and Iran, and just as oil prices had begun falling back to pre-war levels on signs that traffic through the strait was gradually resuming. The UN had been coordinating a multi-nation plan to ensure safe passage for commercial shipping; that effort is now on hold pending an investigation. Separately, a separate report indicates US Vice-President JD Vance recently held direct talks with Iran at a luxury Swiss resort, signalling ongoing backchannel diplomacy despite the tensions.

**Why it matters:** The safety of the Strait of Hormuz remains the single most critical variable for global energy markets, and any escalation here could send oil prices spiking again.

🏷️ Geopolitics / Energy

### #5 | Kevin Warsh's First Day as Fed Chair Sends Markets Higher

Markets rallied on Thursday as Kevin Warsh took the helm as the new Federal Reserve chair, succeeding Jerome Powell. The Dow Jones Industrial Average gained over 400 points, and the S&P 500 rose 1.4%, driven by optimism that Warsh may take a more market-friendly approach to monetary policy. The rally came despite ongoing concerns about inflation — which rose to its highest in three years — and a global tech sell-off that saw SpaceX shares plunge over 30% from their IPO peak, briefly wiping out Elon Musk's trillionaire status. Investors are closely watching Warsh's first policy meeting for signals on interest rate direction. The tech sector rout, driven by anxiety over AI capital spending and doubts about long-term profitability, remains a key headwind.

**Why it matters:** Warsh's appointment marks a new era for US monetary policy at a time when inflation, interest rates, and the tech investment cycle are all at critical inflection points.
