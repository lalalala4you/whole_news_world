## 🌍 Top 5 Global Stories

### #1 | Iran Launches Missiles at Israel, Israel Retaliates — Trump Pushes for Ceasefire
In a dramatic escalation, Iran fired missiles at Israel on Sunday, which Tehran called "the beginning of a full week of continuous strikes." Israel responded with airstrikes on Iranian targets shortly after. US President Donald Trump told both countries to stop "shooting" and claimed both sides want an "immediate ceasefire," with "final peace negotiations" reportedly underway. Israeli media later reported the IDF halted its strikes, and Iran announced an end to its attacks — though it warned it would resume if Israel strikes Lebanon.

**Why it matters:** A direct Iran-Israel military exchange risks dragging the entire Middle East into a broader conflict, with oil prices already spiking and global markets rattled.

### #2 | Powerful 7.8-Magnitude Earthquake Strikes Southern Philippines, At Least 32 Dead
A magnitude-7.8 earthquake hit the southern Philippines, triggering small tsunami waves that reached Indonesia and Japan. At least 32 people are confirmed dead, with the city of Digos particularly hard-hit. Dramatic footage showed schoolchildren screaming and taking cover as the ground shook violently. Rescue operations are ongoing as aftershocks continue to rattle the region.

**Why it matters:** One of the strongest quakes to hit the region in years, with trans-national tsunami alerts underscoring the Pacific Ring of Fire's persistent threat to millions.

### #3 | Xi Jinping Arrives in Pyongyang — First Visit in Seven Years
Chinese President Xi Jinping received a colourful welcome in Pyongyang for his first visit to North Korea since 2019. The trip is widely seen as an effort by Beijing to reassert influence over a strategically vital yet increasingly unpredictable ally, especially as Pyongyang has deepened its military and diplomatic ties with Russia. Kim Jong-un personally greeted Xi at the airport.

**Why it matters:** The visit signals Beijing's concern about losing leverage over North Korea to Moscow, with potential knock-on effects for regional security and nuclear diplomacy.

🏷️ Asia / Geopolitics

### #4 | Zelenskyy's European Allies Set Five Conditions for Ukraine Peace Talks
President Zelenskyy met with close European allies in London, where they outlined five specific conditions for opening peace negotiations. While details remain guarded, the framework focuses on security guarantees, territorial integrity, and accountability mechanisms. The meeting came amid reports that Zelenskyy previously met Russian oligarch Roman Abramovich in Kyiv as a backchannel to Putin.

**Why it matters:** After more than four years of war, European allies are coalescing around a concrete diplomatic off-ramp — but the gap between Kyiv's and Moscow's positions remains vast.

🏷️ Europe / Ukraine War

### #5 | Global Stock Markets Plunge on Tech Sell-Off and Middle East Crisis
Markets in Europe and Asia tumbled as a tech sector sell-off combined with the Iran-Israel military exchange to send investors fleeing risk. Oil prices surged on supply disruption fears, while safe-haven assets like gold and the yen strengthened. The sell-off follows weeks of tech valuation concerns and adds economic jitters to an already tense geopolitical moment.

**Why it matters:** A simultaneous tech correction and Middle East shock is a worst-case cocktail for global markets — and central banks have limited room to respond with inflation still above targets.
