## 🌍 Top 5 Global Stories

### #1 | US and Iran Move Toward Draft Peace Agreement as Strait of Hormuz Crisis Intensifies

A draft memorandum of understanding between the United States and Iran has emerged, linking the reopening of the strategically vital Strait of Hormuz to sanctions relief, according to a June 14 report. This follows weeks of escalating military exchanges, including US strikes on Iranian radar sites, drone command facilities, and the island of Qeshm, as well as Iranian retaliation against a US base in Kuwait. The strait — through which roughly one-fifth of global oil supply transits — has been effectively closed by Iranian forces, sending crude prices sharply higher and rattling global markets. Diplomats from both sides are reportedly working to finalize terms ahead of the G7 summit in France, though Iran's Foreign Ministry has denied ongoing nuclear negotiations.

**Why it matters:** The fate of the Strait of Hormuz directly affects global energy security, shipping, and the world economy — making this the most consequential geopolitical flashpoint of 2026.

🏷️ Geopolitics / Energy

### #2 | G7 Summit Set to Open in France With Iran Crisis, Ukraine War, and Trade Tensions on Agenda

The G7 summit convenes June 16–17 in France, hosted by President Emmanuel Macron, with leaders from the US, UK, Canada, Japan, Germany, Italy, and France facing an unusually packed agenda. The White House has confirmed a bilateral meeting between President Donald Trump and Indian Prime Minister Narendra Modi on the sidelines — a significant diplomatic moment amid strained US-India relations following a US Navy strike on an oil tanker that killed three Indian sailors. Key topics include the Iran crisis, the ongoing war in Ukraine, EU sanctions on Russia, and rising US-China trade tensions. The European Union's Foreign Affairs Council meets separately today in Luxembourg, while the European Parliament begins its plenary session in Strasbourg.

**Why it matters:** The G7 remains the premier forum for Western economic and security coordination; outcomes here will shape global diplomacy through the rest of 2026.

🏷️ Diplomacy / International Relations

### #3 | Israel Strikes Southern Beirut as Middle East Violence Deepens

Israeli artillery and warplanes struck southern Beirut on June 14, in what has become the deepest Israeli incursion into Lebanese territory in over 26 years. The strikes followed the earlier capture of Beaufort Castle by Israeli forces and come despite previous ceasefire frameworks. Simultaneously, Israel is reportedly deepening its strategic relationship with the breakaway state of Somaliland, seeking a Red Sea military foothold as regional alliances shift. The humanitarian situation in Lebanon continues to deteriorate, with the UN warning that essential services and healthcare are being targeted as hostilities between Israeli forces and Hezbollah persist.

**Why it matters:** A multi-front Middle East war — Iran, Lebanon, and Red Sea — risks destabilizing the entire region and drawing in major powers.

🏷️ Conflict / Middle East

### #4 | Reports Cast Doubt on US Ability to Sustain a Conflict With China

Two newly released reports raise serious questions about whether the United States could sustain a prolonged military conflict with China, the South China Morning Post reported on June 14. A US government watchdog has flagged ageing aircraft fleets, critical spare-part shortages, maintenance backlogs, expertise gaps, and significant delays in fielding advanced refueling tankers. Meanwhile, China's military is not standing still: the People's Liberation Army Navy is deploying a new massive 155mm artillery gun, and Chinese military scientists have proposed strategies to destroy US carrier groups from distances of up to 3,000 kilometers. The reports come as both nations continue to posture across the Taiwan Strait and South China Sea.

**Why it matters:** The shifting military balance in the Pacific has profound implications for global security, trade routes, and the credibility of US defense commitments to allies.

🏷️ Defense / US-China

### #5 | Global Oil Prices Spike as Hormuz Closure Rattles Energy Markets

Oil prices have surged dramatically following the effective closure of the Strait of Hormuz by Iranian forces, with Brent crude reportedly crossing well above $100 per barrel. Airlines across Asia and Europe have been forced to cut capacity due to severe jet fuel shortages, while shipping costs have soared, sending ripple effects through global supply chains. The International Monetary Fund had projected global growth at 3.3% for 2026, but prolonged disruption to Persian Gulf energy exports threatens to upend that forecast. Central banks in major economies are monitoring the situation closely, with emergency meetings being discussed amid fears of a return to stagflationary conditions not seen since the 1970s.

**Why it matters:** The Hormuz crisis is the single largest threat to the post-pandemic global economic recovery, with potential knock-on effects for employment, inflation, and political stability worldwide.
