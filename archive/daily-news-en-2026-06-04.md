### #1 | US-Iran War Spirals as Iranian Drones Kill One at Kuwait Airport, US House Votes to Curb Trump's War Powers

In the most dramatic escalation since the US-Iran war began in February, Iranian drones struck Kuwait International Airport on Wednesday, killing an Indian national and injuring more than 60 others — including damage to diplomatic missions. Kuwait expelled two Iranian diplomats within 24 hours and summoned Iran's chargé d'affaires. The IRGC denied responsibility, blaming a US missile interceptor malfunction, but US Central Command called the strike a "deliberate, calculated and unjustified attack." Meanwhile, the US House of Representatives passed a war powers resolution (215-208) seeking to force Trump to withdraw forces or seek Congressional authorization — the first successful bipartisan rebuke after three failed attempts, with four Republicans crossing the aisle. Trump dismissed the resolution as unconstitutional but told reporters a deal with Iran could be finalized "as soon as this weekend," adding cryptically that "in theory they're pretty close to signing a paper."

**Why it matters:** The collision of military escalation, Congressional revolt, and stalled diplomacy makes this the most volatile moment in US-Iran relations since the war began — with global oil markets and Gulf security hanging in the balance.

### #2 | Israel and Lebanon Agree to Renew Ceasefire with "Pilot" Security Zones, Contingent on Hezbollah Halting Attacks

The United States announced that Israel and Lebanon have agreed to implement a renewed ceasefire, establishing US-guided "pilot zones" inside southern Lebanon where the Lebanese Armed Forces would take exclusive control and Hezbollah operatives would be banned. The agreement — reached after the fourth round of Washington-brokered talks — is explicitly "contingent on a complete cessation" of Hezbollah attacks. This follows a volatile week in which Israeli strikes killed at least nine in southern Lebanon and Hezbollah fired rockets into northern Israel, testing a partial truce agreed just days earlier. Hezbollah has not officially commented, and one of its political council members told the BBC the group does "not recognise" the Washington talks. The next round is scheduled for June 22, with the aim of reaching a "comprehensive agreement."

**Why it matters:** The fragile deal represents the most concrete diplomatic progress toward containing the Israel-Hezbollah front of the broader Middle East war — but Hezbollah's non-participation leaves it on a knife's edge.

### #3 | Ukrainian Drones Strike St Petersburg Hours Before Putin's Flagship Economic Forum

Ukraine carried out a bold drone assault on St Petersburg on Wednesday, hitting an oil terminal and a Baltic Fleet naval base in Kronstadt just hours before the opening of Russia's premier international investment event — the St Petersburg Economic Forum, once dubbed the "Russian Davos." Russian air defenses shot down 59 drones overnight, with three St Petersburg districts hit and Pulkovo Airport temporarily closed. No deaths were reported, but the symbolism was devastating: thick black smoke billowed over Russia's second city as Vladimir Putin prepared to address thousands of guests from 130 countries, including an unusual low-key US delegation. President Zelensky confirmed the strikes, saying Ukraine's "plan of long-range sanctions is being implemented exactly as it is needed to bring peace closer." The corvette Boikiy of the Baltic Fleet was reportedly struck.

**Why it matters:** Ukraine's ability to strike Russia's second-largest city — 1,200 km from its border — during Putin's prestige economic event marks a dramatic evolution in Kyiv's long-range strike capability and psychological warfare.

### #4 | South China Sea Enters "Dredging War" as Vietnam, Philippines Join China's Land Reclamation Race

A new front has opened in the South China Sea: a "dredging war." China has transformed Antelope Reef in the Paracel Islands from a submerged speck into a 6-sq-km island with buildings and a potential military runway — all in just six months. But for the first time, other claimants are copying China's playbook. Vietnam has been pumping sand around at least 20 reefs over three years, creating 11 new harbours and now building military-grade navigation beacons, controlling over 11 sq km of reclaimed land. The Philippines has also begun its own smaller-scale reclamation. The trend marks a stark shift from diplomatic protest to physical fait accompli, as nations adopt an "if you can't beat them, join them" approach. China banned four New Zealand MPs this week over a Taiwan visit, underscoring Beijing's broader assertiveness in the region.

**Why it matters:** The spread of land reclamation by multiple claimants fundamentally reshapes the South China Sea's physical and strategic map — turning disputed waters into permanently occupied territory and raising the stakes for future conflict.

### #5 | SpaceX Targets Record $1.75 Trillion IPO — Largest Stock Market Debut in History

Elon Musk's SpaceX has set a suggested share price of $135 ahead of its Nasdaq listing on June 12, valuing the company at roughly $1.75 trillion — which would make it the largest initial public offering in history. The company aims to raise $75 billion, nearly triple the current record held by Saudi Aramco ($25.6bn in 2019). The valuation has been supercharged by SpaceX's acquisition of xAI (maker of the Grok chatbot), transforming what was once a launch-and-satellite company into an AI, social media, and space internet conglomerate. However, skeptics note the company posted a net loss of $4.3bn on $4.7bn in revenue in Q1 2026 and carries $60.5bn in debt. Analysts warn the valuation is "incredibly rich," priced on future AI earnings rather than current performance. OpenAI is also reportedly considering going public this year, as AI funding mania reshapes global capital markets.

**Why it matters:** The largest IPO in history — powered by AI hype more than aerospace fundamentals — will test whether the AI-driven tech bubble has room to inflate further or is approaching its limit.
