## 🌍 Top 5 Global Stories

### #1 | Israel-Hezbollah Ceasefire Agreed as US-Iran Peace Deal Frays

A fragile Israel-Hezbollah ceasefire was declared on Friday, brokered by the United States, following an overnight Israeli bombing campaign across southern Lebanon that killed 47 people, including women and children. Hezbollah ambushed an Israeli unit in response, destroying three tanks and killing four Israeli soldiers — including a battalion commander — in one of the deadliest single exchanges since the US-Iran war began. The flareup cast serious doubt over the US-Iran peace memorandum, with JD Vance abruptly pulling out of implementation talks in Switzerland and Iran warning that any breach "will be attributed to the US." More than 3,900 people have been killed and roughly one million displaced in Lebanon alone; the US State Department says direct Lebanon-Israel talks will resume in Washington next week.

**Why it matters:** The ceasefire is the linchpin of Trump's broader Iran peace deal — if it collapses, the entire regional settlement could unravel, dragging the Middle East back into full-scale war.

### #2 | Trump-Meloni Feud Erupts as US President Claims Italian PM "Begged" for G7 Photo

Donald Trump claimed in an Italian TV interview that Giorgia Meloni "begged me to take a picture with her" at the G7 summit in Évian, sparking fury across Italy's entire political spectrum. Meloni responded on Instagram saying Trump "totally invented" the story, adding: "I don't know why the US president behaves this way towards his allies. It's unfortunate he doesn't show the same determination towards the west's enemies." Italy's Foreign Minister Antonio Tajani immediately cancelled a planned trip to Washington, calling Trump's words "serious and offensive." The incident follows their April falling-out over Italy's refusal to support the US-Israeli war in Iran, and the undersecretary to the PM's office accused Trump of "wrecking the historic relations between the United States and Europe." In a separate remark, when a "historian" compared Trump to Hitler, Mao, and Stalin, the president responded: "Sounds good to me!"

**Why it matters:** The public breakdown between Trump and one of his last remaining European allies signals a deepening transatlantic rift at a moment when Western unity is critical to the Iran peace process.

🏷️ US Politics / Europe / Diplomacy

### #3 | UK Prime Minister Starmer Faces Mounting Calls to Set Exit Timetable

Keir Starmer is under intense pressure from senior Labour MPs and cabinet ministers — reportedly including the transport secretary — to set a timetable for his departure from Downing Street, following a damaging by-election defeat in Makerfield won by Andy Burnham. The leadership crisis intensified as Starmer vowed to fight any challenge, but discussions about his future have moved from backbench murmurs to open cabinet-level conversations. The political turmoil was compounded by a major rail disaster near Bedford on Thursday, where two trains collided, killing the driver and injuring 89 people — 11 with "very serious" injuries — in what was declared a major incident.

**Why it matters:** The potential collapse of a G7 leader's government amid domestic crises adds significant uncertainty to European politics at a fragile geopolitical moment.

🏷️ UK Politics / Europe

### #4 | China Arrests US Academic at Conference on Espionage Charges

A US academic has been arrested by Chinese authorities while attending a conference in China, formally charged with "engaging in espionage activities." Details remain scarce, but the arrest marks a sharp escalation in US-China tensions, coming amid an already strained relationship over technology restrictions, trade disputes, and military posturing in the South China Sea. The US State Department has not yet issued a formal response.

**Why it matters:** The detention of an American academic on espionage charges risks triggering a new diplomatic crisis between the world's two largest economies at a time when both are navigating separate major conflicts.

🏷️ US-China / Diplomacy / Security

### #5 | US Halts HIV Programme Funding in South Africa

The United States has announced it will stop funding HIV treatment and prevention programmes in South Africa, where more than eight million people live with HIV — the largest HIV-positive population of any country in the world. The decision threatens to reverse decades of progress in combating the epidemic in sub-Saharan Africa, which has relied heavily on US-funded initiatives like PEPFAR for antiretroviral drug distribution and prevention programmes.

**Why it matters:** Cutting HIV funding to the country with the world's highest caseload could trigger a public health catastrophe affecting millions, with ripple effects across the entire African continent.

🏷️ Global Health / Africa / US Policy
