## 🌍 Top 5 Global Stories

### #1 | US–Iran War Spirals into Multi-Front Regional Conflict

Iran's Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps launched drone and missile strikes against the U.S. Fifth Fleet in Bahrain, retaliating for American strikes on a water plant and telecommunications mast in Sirik a day earlier. Simultaneously, Iran fired missiles at Jordan's Muwaffaq Salti Air Base — five were intercepted over Azraq with no casualties — and Kuwait's army activated air defenses against "hostile" aerial targets crossing its airspace. In response, U.S. Central Command launched airstrikes on targets across Iran, though President Trump told Fox News from the Situation Room that "the bombing will stop shortly," signaling possible de-escalation.

**Why it matters:** The conflict has metastasized from a bilateral U.S.–Iran confrontation into a regional war drawing in Gulf Arab states, Jordan, and potentially triggering a broader Middle Eastern conflagration.

🏷️ War / Geopolitics

### #2 | Xi Jinping Makes Historic Visit to North Korea, Honors War Dead

President Xi Jinping and First Lady Peng Liyuan presided over a solemn wreath-laying ceremony at the Korea War monument in Pyongyang, in what state media described as evoking "eternal historical memory." The visit — Xi's first to North Korea in years — comes at a time of heightened geopolitical flux in East Asia and signals Beijing's intent to reinforce its alliance with Pyongyang amid global instability.

**Why it matters:** Xi's highly symbolic trip strengthens the Beijing–Pyongyang axis and reshapes the Northeast Asian security landscape at a moment when global attention is diverted to the Middle East.

🏷️ Diplomacy / East Asia

### #3 | Israel Strikes Southern Lebanon, Killing 12 as Multi-Front War Grinds On

Israeli airstrikes across southern Lebanon killed at least 12 people and wounded 13, including two paramedics, as the 2026 Lebanon war continued without respite. In a striking development underscoring the toll of the conflict, Lebanon nonetheless inaugurated its second international airport — Rene Mouawad Airport — operating despite the ongoing hostilities. The twin realities of war and resilience capture Lebanon's precarious position.

**Why it matters:** The Lebanon front remains active and deadly alongside the Iran crisis, stretching Israeli military resources and deepening the humanitarian catastrophe across the Levant.

🏷️ War / Middle East

### #4 | UN Security Council Convenes Emergency Debate as Chief Warns Crisis "Reverberates Across Borders"

The UN Security Council held a high-level debate on the Middle East on Wednesday, with Secretary-General António Guterres warning that escalation "reverberates across borders and continents." The session, convened against the backdrop of U.S.–Iran combat operations and the Lebanon war, focused on advancing political solutions amid what the UN described as the worst regional crisis in decades. Diplomats scrambled for a ceasefire framework even as bombs fell.

**Why it matters:** Multilateral diplomacy is racing to catch up with a rapidly deteriorating security environment that threatens global energy markets, trade routes, and the post-WWII international order.

🏷️ Diplomacy / United Nations

### #5 | India's Fuel Subsidy Bill Soars to ₹1.23 Lakh Crore as West Asia Crisis Hits Home

The Indian government disclosed it has provided nearly ₹1.23 lakh crore (approximately $14.8 billion USD) in support to state-run oil companies to freeze fuel prices for 78 days, a direct consequence of the West Asia conflict disrupting global crude supplies. The Fertiliser Ministry simultaneously sought a near-100% increase in subsidy allocation, totalling an additional ₹3.4 lakh crore, as import costs surged. The dual shock underscores how the Middle Eastern war is ricocheting into the domestic economies of major emerging markets.

**Why it matters:** The economic contagion from the Gulf conflict is now measurably straining the fiscal positions of the world's most populous nation, with knock-on effects for global growth and inflation.

🏷️ Economy / South Asia
