## 🌍 Top 5 Global Stories

### #1 | US Launches Fresh Strikes on Iran as Peace Deal Hangs in Balance

The US military has launched new strikes on southern Iran, targeting missile launch sites and boats near Bandar Abbas on the Strait of Hormuz. US Central Command said the strikes were taken in "self-defence" and designed to protect American troops, while Iran's foreign ministry said "some progress" has been made in ongoing peace talks but a deal "is not imminent." President Trump had suggested the sides were close to a deal involving a 60-day ceasefire extension, reopening of the Strait of Hormuz, and a framework for negotiating Iran's nuclear programme. Oil prices fell sharply and Asian markets rose on Monday on hopes of an agreement, though Trump later said he had told negotiators "not to rush."

**Why it matters:** The outcome of these negotiations will determine whether the Strait of Hormuz — through which 20% of the world's oil passes — reopens, directly affecting global energy prices and the trajectory of the US-Iran war that began in February.

### #2 | Russia Threatens Systematic Strikes on Kyiv, Tells Foreign Nationals to Leave

Russia has threatened to launch a fresh wave of "systematic strikes" against Kyiv targeting "decision-making centres and command posts," days after carrying out one of the largest aerial assaults on the Ukrainian capital since the war began. Saturday night's barrage killed four people and injured about 100 across Ukraine, using dozens of ballistic and cruise missiles, hundreds of drones, and a hypersonic Oreshnik missile. Russian Foreign Minister Sergei Lavrov urged US Secretary of State Marco Rubio to evacuate American diplomats from Kyiv in a Monday phone call. Ukraine called the threats "shameless blackmail" and urged allies to increase pressure on Moscow.

**Why it matters:** The escalation signals Moscow's intent to intensify urban warfare despite years of fortified Ukrainian air defences, threatening civilian life in the capital and testing Western resolve as attention is divided by the Iran conflict.

🏷️ War / Europe

### #3 | China's Deadliest Coal Mine Disaster in 15 Years Kills 82, Sparks Outrage

At least 82 people were killed and more than 120 injured in a gas explosion at the Liushenyu coal mine in Shanxi province, China's deadliest mining disaster since the 2000s. Initial investigations revealed that Tongzhou Group, the private operator, had committed "serious illegal violations" — including unregistered workers (the number underground was double the official count) and falsified mine blueprints that complicated rescue efforts. The company had received two administrative penalties in 2025 and its mine was previously flagged on a national list of "severe hazards." On China's tightly-controlled internet, an unusual wave of public anger has erupted, with citizens demanding accountability.

**Why it matters:** The disaster exposes deep failures in China's much-touted mine safety reforms, threatening public trust at a time when the government is already managing economic headwinds and geopolitical tensions.

🏷️ China / Disaster

### #4 | Netanyahu Orders Intensified Strikes Against Hezbollah, IDF Hits Bekaa Valley

Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu announced that Israel will "increase the strikes, increase the intensity" against Hezbollah, calling for a "crushing blow" as the IDF launched attacks across Lebanon including the eastern Bekaa Valley — a significant geographic escalation. Hezbollah retaliated with 22 drone and rocket attacks on Israeli positions. Over 400 people have been killed in Lebanon since the 16 April ceasefire was agreed, and more than one million remain displaced. Iran is demanding that any peace deal with the US include a complete ceasefire on all fronts, but Israel's government has resisted linking the Lebanon front to Iran negotiations.

**Why it matters:** The widening of Israel's campaign into eastern Lebanon risks derailing both the fragile Lebanon ceasefire and the broader US-Iran peace talks, prolonging a regional war that has already killed over 3,000 in Lebanon.

🏷️ Middle East / Conflict

### #5 | White House Gunman Had Prior Secret Service Encounters and Mental Health History

Nasire Best, the man who fired shots outside the White House on Saturday evening, had multiple prior run-ins with the Secret Service, including obstructing a White House entry lane in June 2025 and telling agents he was Jesus Christ. He was sent for a mental evaluation but returned in July, was arrested for unlawful entry, and then failed to appear for a court hearing in August — resulting in an outstanding bench warrant. On Saturday, he opened fire at a Secret Service checkpoint near the Eisenhower Executive Office Building, striking a bystander before agents shot and killed him. President Trump was unharmed. This is the second White House security incident in a month, following the gunman at the White House Correspondents' Dinner in April.

**Why it matters:** The shooting raises serious questions about the Secret Service's ability to track and prevent known threats from escalating, exposing gaps in the security net around the US president.
