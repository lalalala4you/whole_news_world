### #1 | US and Iran Exchange Direct Strikes, Ceasefire Collapses

The fragile two-week-old US-Iran ceasefire collapsed as both sides launched retaliatory strikes overnight. Iran struck US military infrastructure in Kuwait and Bahrain after the US hit multiple targets inside Iran. President Trump threatened to "annihilate" Iran, while Vice President JD Vance claimed the US holds all the cards and will win "either way." The UN has paused its Strait of Hormuz evacuation plan following an attack on the cargo ship Ever Lovely, raising fresh fears for global oil shipping lanes. Iran also threatened a "complete halt" to negotiations.

**Why it matters:** A full US-Iran conflict threatens to disrupt the Strait of Hormuz — through which 20% of the world's oil passes — with immediate consequences for global energy prices and supply chains.

### #2 | Venezuela Earthquakes: Death Toll Surpasses 1,400 as Rescue Efforts Continue

Powerful back-to-back earthquakes struck Venezuela on June 25, leaving more than 1,400 dead and thousands injured, with the port city of La Guaira particularly devastated. Rescue workers continue to pull survivors — including an 11-year-old boy and a newborn baby — from the rubble, as desperate families keep vigil outside collapsed buildings. The disaster tests the Trump administration's new western hemisphere policy, with Secretary of State Marco Rubio scrambling to organize an effective US response after the gutting of USAID. Venezuela's interim president has vowed to save "as many as possible."

**Why it matters:** One of the deadliest seismic events in the Americas in recent years, compounding Venezuela's existing humanitarian and political crises.

🏷️ Americas / Natural Disaster

### #3 | Europe Swelters Under Record-Breaking Heatwave

A severe heatwave has broken temperature records across Germany, Denmark, Czech Republic, and Slovakia, with forecasts of 40°C reaching Germany and Poland. Drought fears intensify in Italy, while scientists report that snow and ice on Swiss glaciers are melting at an alarming rate. Thunderstorms linked to the heatwave have also caused major disruption in the UK, delaying more than 900 flights at Heathrow and Gatwick airports. The extreme weather has prompted emergency measures across the continent.

**Why it matters:** The heatwave highlights accelerating climate change impacts on European infrastructure, agriculture, and public health.

🏷️ Europe / Climate

### #5 | Ebola: 300 Patients Unaccounted For in DR Congo as France Confirms First Case

The whereabouts of nearly 300 people with suspected or confirmed Ebola remain unknown in the Democratic Republic of Congo, raising fears of a wider outbreak. In a major escalation of the cross-border health threat, France confirmed its first Ebola case — a doctor who had recently worked in the DRC. The World Health Organization is racing to contain the spread as contact tracing efforts face severe challenges in conflict-affected regions of eastern DRC.

**Why it matters:** An uncontrolled Ebola outbreak reaching Europe signals a potential international public health emergency requiring coordinated global response.
