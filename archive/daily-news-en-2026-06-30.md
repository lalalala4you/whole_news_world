## 🌍 Top 5 Global Stories

### #1 | US-Iran Tensions Flare in Gulf as Trump Claims Peace Talks Set for Doha

The Strait of Hormuz saw a dangerous escalation as US and Iranian forces exchanged fire over the weekend, prompting President Trump to claim — in a social media post — that Iran has agreed to peace talks in Doha scheduled for Tuesday. The fresh hostilities threaten an already fragile interim peace memorandum between the two nations, with Trump also threatening to "annihilate" Iran if further provoked. The UN and regional powers are watching closely, as the Strait of Hormuz remains the world's most critical oil transit choke point.

**Why it matters:** A full-blown US-Iran conflict would disrupt global oil supply, rattle markets, and could draw in Gulf allies and Russia/China.

🏷️ Geopolitics / Middle East

### #2 | UK PM Starmer Unveils £15bn Defence Boost, Axes Road and Energy Projects

Prime Minister Keir Starmer announced an additional £15 billion for UK defence, warning that the country faces increasingly dangerous threats. The package falls short of the £28 billion requested by the Ministry of Defence, and will be partly funded by scrapping some road and energy infrastructure projects. Starmer insisted defence spending "cannot be a bottomless pit" and called on the MoD to "spend better." The announcement comes amid a broader European rearmament push.

**Why it matters:** Signals the UK's serious defence posture shift, but the funding gap with MoD expectations and sacrificed civilian projects will stir political debate.

🏷️ Politics / Defence

### #3 | Venezuela Reels from Devastating Twin Earthquakes — Over 1,700 Dead, Anger at Government Response

Venezuela is in crisis after twin earthquakes devastated coastal regions including La Guaira. Over 1,700 people are confirmed dead with tens of thousands still missing. Rescuers are using dogs, drones, and sound detectors to search rubble, but anger is mounting at the government's slow and inadequate response. A strong aftershock near Caracas on Monday further frayed nerves. The disaster has compounded Venezuela's existing economic and humanitarian struggles.

**Why it matters:** One of the deadliest natural disasters in Latin America in decades, testing an already fragile state's capacity — and the international community's humanitarian response.

🏷️ Disaster / Humanitarian

### #4 | World Cup Shocks: Germany and Netherlands Both Crash Out in Penalty Heartbreak

The 2026 FIFA World Cup delivered its biggest upsets yet as both Germany and the Netherlands were eliminated in the Round of 32 on penalties. Germany lost to Paraguay 5-4 in their first-ever World Cup penalty shootout defeat, piling pressure on manager Julian Nagelsmann. Hours later, the Netherlands fell to Morocco 3-2 on penalties after a dramatic match that saw Cody Gakpo score days after the loss of his unborn son. Meanwhile, Brazil narrowly edged Japan 2-1 thanks to a late Gabriel Martinelli goal.

**Why it matters:** Two European football powerhouses gone before the knockout rounds even properly began — a historic tournament shake-up with Morocco and Paraguay as giant-killers.

🏷️ Sports / World Cup 2026

### #5 | Monaco Bombing: Manhunt for Attacker Who Sent Parcel Bomb to Ukrainian Oligarch

Monaco is in shock after a parcel bomb exploded in the principality, injuring a Ukrainian-born business tycoon and others. Authorities say the device contained bolts and pellets and have classified the incident as an "attempted assassination" rather than terrorism. A manhunt is underway for the unidentified suspect. The brazen attack in one of the world's most secure and wealthy enclaves has raised questions about private security and geopolitical reach.

**Why it matters:** A targeted assassination attempt in Monaco — involving a Ukrainian oligarch — raises spectres of transnational organized crime or state-linked operations spilling into Western Europe.

🏷️ Crime / Europe
