## 🌍 Top 5 Global Stories

### #1 | Trump Declares "War Ended," Announces Nuclear Deal with Iran as Strikes Called Off

US President Donald Trump declared on Thursday that the United States had "ended the war with Iran," announcing a landmark nuclear agreement and cancelling planned military strikes that had been scheduled for the same evening. Speaking from the Oval Office and at a Georgia rally, Trump said Iran had "agreed never to have a nuclear weapon" and committed not to "purchase, develop in any way, any shape, or form a nuclear weapon." He described the accord as "a very strong memorandum of understanding" but acknowledged it remained "a little conceptual," adding that documents were in "pretty final shape" and a formal signing ceremony in Europe could take place within days, with Vice President JD Vance representing him. Iran's foreign ministry, however, pushed back, calling reports of a finalized deal "merely speculation," confirming that Qatar and Pakistan were mediating but that Washington's shifting positions had complicated the diplomatic process. The announcement came after three days of what Trump called sustained military action involving over 50,000 US troops under CENTCOM command, which Trump credited with forcing Tehran to the negotiating table.

**Why it matters:** A US-Iran nuclear agreement would be the most consequential Middle East diplomatic breakthrough in decades, with immediate ripple effects on global oil markets, energy security, and great-power competition in the Gulf.

### #2 | US Admits Attacking Third Commercial Vessel in the Gulf; Blockade on Iranian Oil Tightens

US Central Command admitted to disabling a third commercial vessel — this time with an Indian crew — in the Gulf, saying the tanker violated an American-imposed blockade on Iranian oil exports. The incident marks a significant escalation in the ongoing naval enforcement campaign around the Strait of Hormuz, through which roughly one-fifth of global oil supplies transit. Iran's foreign ministry warned that the Strait had become "less secure because of US actions." The attacks on commercial shipping, even as Trump announced a prospective deal, underscore the continued volatility of the maritime corridor and the disconnect between diplomatic rhetoric and military realities on the water.

**Why it matters:** Attacks on commercial vessels in the Strait of Hormuz threaten global energy supply chains and risk drawing additional naval powers — including India, whose crew was directly affected — into an already explosive regional confrontation.

🏷️ Maritime Security / Energy

### #3 | Israeli Military Escalates in Lebanon Despite April Ceasefire Agreement

Despite a ceasefire agreement reached in mid-April, the Israeli military continues to escalate operations in southern Lebanon, with new strikes and ground movements reported this week. The escalation undermines international diplomatic efforts led by France and the EU, and raises fears that the fragile truce could collapse entirely. The EU and France have been pressing for renewed peace talks in Paris, but the deteriorating situation on the ground threatens to spiral into a broader regional conflict involving Hezbollah and, potentially, Iran-aligned proxies across the Levant.

**Why it matters:** A collapse of the Lebanon ceasefire would re-ignite a front that directly threatens Israel's northern border, Lebanon's already fragile state, and the broader US-led diplomatic framework across the Middle East.

🏷️ Middle East Conflict

### #4 | EU Sharpens Trade Sword Against China While Pushing Tech Sovereignty

Tensions between Brussels and Beijing continued to escalate this week, with the European Union threatening a trade war that commentators in the South China Morning Post described as a "strategic farce." Simultaneously, the EU Commission proposed specific criteria for a "sovereign cloud" to reduce dependence on non-European tech infrastructure, and launched a new EU Innovation Platform to connect startups with funding. The twin moves — trade pressure and tech self-reliance — reflect Brussels' deepening ambition to decouple critical digital infrastructure from both American and Chinese providers. The European Sustainable Energy Week, also underway in Brussels, highlighted the EU's parallel push for green energy independence.

**Why it matters:** The EU's simultaneous trade offensive against China and technology sovereignty drive signals a structural shift in global trade and tech architecture, with the world's largest trading bloc actively fragmenting rather than integrating supply chains.

🏷️ Trade / Technology

### #5 | Japan's Ruling LDP Wins Historic Supermajority as Quad Ministers Converge in New Delhi

Japan's Liberal Democratic Party (LDP) has become the first party in postwar Japanese history to win a two-thirds majority in the House of Representatives, securing 316 out of 465 seats in the 2026 general election. The supermajority gives Prime Minister Ishiba an unprecedented mandate to pursue constitutional revision and accelerate defense spending increases. Meanwhile, Quad foreign ministers from the US, Japan, India, and Australia met in New Delhi this week to reaffirm their strategic partnership, with discussions focused on countering China's growing influence in the Indo-Pacific, semiconductor supply chain resilience, and freedom of navigation in the South China Sea. The convergence of Japan's political mandate and Quad's renewed cohesion marks a significant strengthening of the democratic alliance architecture in the Pacific.

**Why it matters:** A militarily and constitutionally empowered Japan, backed by a reinvigorated Quad, fundamentally reshapes the balance of power in the Indo-Pacific at a time of heightened US-China strategic competition.
