## 🌍 Top 5 Global Stories

### #1 | US-Iran War Fuels Global Energy Crisis as Oil Prices Surge

The US-Iran conflict has sent shockwaves through global energy markets as the effective closure of the Strait of Hormuz — a chokepoint for roughly one-fifth of the world's oil and gas — drives fuel prices to multi-year highs. US gasoline now averages $4.24 per gallon, up sharply from $2.98 before hostilities began. President Trump is preparing to invoke the Cold War-era Defense Production Act to direct $700 million into reviving domestic coal production, framing it as a national security measure. Public opposition to the war is mounting, with a New York Times/Siena poll showing 64% of registered voters believe the president was wrong to go to war, including 73% of independents. The crisis has also complicated US diplomatic bandwidth, with Kyiv publicly acknowledging that Washington "is fully focused on the issue of Iran."

**Why it matters:** The Iran conflict is reshaping global energy flows, straining alliance diplomacy, and injecting extraordinary volatility into markets from Tokyo to London.

### #2 | Israel and Lebanon Agree to Partial Ceasefire as US Mediates

Israel and Lebanon reached a US-brokered partial ceasefire agreement on Monday, under which Israel would refrain from bombing Beirut and Hezbollah would halt attacks on Israel. The deal — reached after a fourth round of Washington-mediated talks — also envisions "pilot" security zones inside southern Lebanon where the Lebanese Armed Forces would take exclusive control, excluding all non-state actors. However, Hezbollah was not a party to the negotiations, and its leader Naim Qassem later dismissed the arrangement as "surrender." Follow-up talks are scheduled for June 22 with the stated goal of reaching a comprehensive agreement.

**Why it matters:** The fragile deal tests whether US diplomacy can de-escalate the Lebanon front even as Washington remains militarily engaged with Iran.

### #3 | South China Sea "Dredging War" Enters Dangerous New Phase

China has transformed Antelope Reef in the Paracel Islands from a barely visible speck into a 6-square-kilometre island in just six months — deploying its massive fleet of cutter suction dredgers at what analysts call record-breaking speed. Satellite imagery suggests the straight-edged new land may accommodate a military-grade runway. In response, Vietnam has accelerated its own dredging operations across at least 20 reefs, creating 11 new harbours and more than 11 square kilometres of reclaimed land. Other claimants, including the Philippines, are also expanding their footprint. "Grab what you can while you can" has become the de facto posture, according to the BBC.

**Why it matters:** The militarisation of disputed reefs raises the risk of miscalculation in one of the world's most contested waterways, through which some $3 trillion in trade passes annually.

### #4 | China's Worst Coal Mine Disaster in 15 Years Exposes Deep Safety Failures

A major coal mine accident in China has claimed dozens of lives, marking the deadliest mining disaster the country has seen in 15 years. Investigations have uncovered secret tunnels and unregistered workers at the site — a grim echo of darker days in China's coal industry. The tragedy comes at an awkward moment for Beijing, which is simultaneously pursuing an ambitious green energy pivot while also grappling with energy security concerns. Chinese authorities have launched a safety crackdown, but the disaster has reignited scrutiny of enforcement gaps in the sector.

**Why it matters:** The disaster underscores the tension between China's energy transition ambitions and the deadly realities of its continued reliance on coal.

🏷️ China / Industry & Safety

### #5 | Deadly Delhi Fire Kills 21, Mostly Foreign Medical Tourists

At least 21 people were killed and dozens more injured when a fire engulfed a multi-storey building in the Malviya Nagar area of south Delhi. Many of the victims were foreign nationals from South Asian and African countries who had travelled to India for medical treatment or to accompany relatives undergoing care. The building allegedly operated as an unlicensed bed-and-breakfast catering to medical tourists near a private hospital. Eyewitnesses described harrowing scenes of people jumping from upper floors and victims found unconscious in washrooms. Prime Minister Narendra Modi expressed condolences and announced compensation for victims' families. Authorities are investigating whether the building had the necessary permits.

**Why it matters:** The tragedy highlights persistent building safety enforcement failures in India and the vulnerability of the country's booming medical tourism sector.
