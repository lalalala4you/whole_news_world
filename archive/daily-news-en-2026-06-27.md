## 🌍 Top 5 Global Stories

### #1 | US Strikes Iran After Attack on Singapore-Flagged Cargo Ship in Strait of Hormuz
The US military conducted strikes on Iranian missile and drone storage facilities and coastal radar positions on Friday, in retaliation for a drone attack on the Singapore-flagged cargo ship Ever Lovely in the Strait of Hormuz a day earlier. Vice President JD Vance declared that "violence will be met with violence," while Iran's IRGC claimed it had already retaliated by striking US military positions in the region. The attack on the Ever Lovely — which was following the UKMTO's recommended route — caused the UN to pause the planned evacuation of more than 11,000 sailors stranded in the waterway since hostilities began in February. The fragile June 17 ceasefire between the US and Iran now hangs by a thread, threatening to reignite a conflict that has already choked global oil and commodity shipments through the vital trade corridor.

**Why it matters:** The Strait of Hormuz handles roughly one-fifth of global oil transit — any escalation threatens to send energy prices soaring and disrupt supply chains worldwide.

### #2 | Venezuela Earthquakes: Death Toll Passes 920 as Rescue Efforts Intensify
The death toll from devastating earthquakes in Venezuela has surged past 920, with hundreds more feared trapped beneath collapsed buildings as international rescue teams begin arriving in the country. Interim President Delcy Rodríguez vowed to save "as many as possible" amid growing public anger over the government's slow response and limited resources. Heartbreaking stories are emerging, including a mother who died shielding her daughter from falling debris. Aerial footage has revealed widespread destruction along Venezuela's coastal regions, with survivors describing the quakes as "brutal and fast." The disaster adds to the humanitarian strain on a nation already grappling with political and economic crises.

**Why it matters:** It is one of the deadliest natural disasters in Latin America in decades, requiring massive international humanitarian mobilization at a time of stretched global resources.

🏷️ Natural Disaster / Latin America / Humanitarian

### #3 | Europe's Deadly Heatwave Breaks Records, Halts Public Life
A brutal heatwave sweeping across Europe has broken June temperature records in Germany (41.3°C in Saarbrücken), the UK (37.1°C), Belgium, and the Netherlands, with over 150 million people facing temperatures above 35°C. At least four toddlers have died in heat-related car incidents in France, while Spain's monitoring system has counted 327 heat-linked fatalities since Sunday. Major events have been cancelled — including Paris Pride, the Dutch Defqon.1 music festival, and the Hamburg Half Marathon — as overwhelmed hospitals brace for more casualties. Switzerland's Beznau nuclear plant was forced offline because river water became too warm for cooling, and researchers warn that Alpine glaciers are melting at near-record rates, with winter snow reserves expected to vanish by Monday.

**Why it matters:** Europe is the world's fastest-warming continent, heating at twice the global average — this heatwave is a stark preview of the new climate normal, with cascading impacts on health, energy, and infrastructure.

🏷️ Climate / Europe / Public Health

### #4 | Israel and Lebanon Sign US-Brokered Framework Agreement
Israel and Lebanon signed a 14-point framework agreement in Washington on Friday after several days of US-brokered negotiations, outlining a path toward lasting peace and security. The deal affirms each nation's right to "live in peace" and calls for the Lebanese Armed Forces to restore sovereign authority over all Lebanese territory — pending the disarmament of Hezbollah and other non-state armed groups. However, Hezbollah is not party to the agreement, and Israeli Prime Minister Netanyahu reiterated that Israeli forces would remain in the roughly 5% of Lebanese territory they currently occupy until disarmament is verified. The signing is a diplomatic milestone, but on the ground, near-daily cross-border strikes continue despite multiple previous ceasefires.

**Why it matters:** The agreement represents the most significant diplomatic breakthrough between Israel and Lebanon in years, but its success hinges entirely on Hezbollah's cooperation — a major uncertainty given the group's ties to Iran.

🏷️ Middle East / Diplomacy / Security

### #5 | Global Markets Tumble as Economic Headwinds Multiply
Asian stock markets slid sharply on Friday as technology shares led a broad sell-off, compounding fears of a global economic slowdown. Volkswagen is reportedly planning to cut up to 100,000 jobs and shut plants in Germany, signaling deep trouble in Europe's industrial heartland. Canada's economy is facing its own reckoning, with analysts warning of structural weaknesses compared to other wealthy nations. The Strait of Hormuz crisis, Europe's heatwave-driven disruptions, and ongoing trade uncertainties are converging to create an increasingly volatile global economic picture heading into the second half of 2026.

**Why it matters:** A simultaneous confluence of geopolitical risk, climate disruption, and industrial contraction is testing the resilience of the global economy in ways not seen since the pandemic era.
