## 🌍 Top 5 Global Stories

### #1 | US House Votes to Curb Trump's Iran War Powers

The US House of Representatives passed a war powers resolution on Wednesday, voting 215–208 to require President Trump to withdraw forces or seek congressional approval for the Iran conflict. Four Republicans joined all Democrats in backing the measure, prompting Trump to brand the vote "unpatriotic" and accuse opponents of undermining negotiations. The resolution is largely symbolic — it would need to clear the Republican-controlled Senate and survive legal challenges — but it marks the fourth formal attempt by the House to rein in the president's war authority, well past the 60-day legal deadline for congressional authorization. Public opposition to the war has surged, with a New York Times/Siena poll showing 64% of registered voters believe Trump was wrong to go to war with Iran.

**Why it matters:** The vote represents the most significant congressional challenge to Trump's war powers since the Iran conflict began, exposing growing Republican dissent and mounting public pressure for a diplomatic off-ramp.

### #2 | South China Sea Crisis Escalates as China Builds Antelope Reef at Record Speed

China has transformed Antelope Reef in the Paracel Islands from a submerged atoll into a 6-square-kilometre artificial island in just six months — a dredging speed experts call a likely world record. Satellite imagery suggests China may be building another military-grade runway, even though it already operates an airstrip on nearby Woody Island. The rapid build-up has triggered a regional dredging race: Vietnam has created over 11 square kilometres of reclaimed land across 20 reefs, while the Philippines is expanding its runway on Pagasa (Thitu) Island. Analysts say China's Antelope Reef operation is a pointed message to Vietnam, whose quieter diplomatic approach masks increasingly assertive actions at sea.

**Why it matters:** The accelerating island-building competition is reshaping the strategic map of one of the world's most contested waterways, drawing in ASEAN claimants, the US, Japan, and Australia.

### #3 | Hezbollah Rejects US-Brokered Israel-Lebanon Ceasefire

Hezbollah leader Naim Qassem issued a strongly worded rejection of a renewed ceasefire agreed between Israel and Lebanon, calling the US-mediated deal "futile" and "humiliating." The agreement — reached after a fourth round of talks in Washington — would create "pilot security zones" in southern Lebanon where the Lebanese Armed Forces would take exclusive control from Hezbollah, while requiring the group to stop attacking Israel. Israeli Defence Minister Israel Katz said the military would "continue its fire and operations on the ground" for now, and Lebanese media reported multiple Israeli strikes across southern Lebanon on Thursday. The US is pushing for a comprehensive deal ahead of June 22 follow-up talks, but Hezbollah's rejection underscores the fragility of any agreement that excludes the group directly.

**Why it matters:** The ceasefire collapse complicates Trump's broader push for a Middle East peace deal with Iran — which insists any settlement must include Lebanon — and threatens to prolong a conflict destabilising the region.

🏷️ Middle East

### #4 | Anthropic Calls for Global AI Development Freeze

AI company Anthropic — maker of the Claude models and the powerful Mythos system — proposed a worldwide pause on building the most advanced AI systems, warning that the latest models are beginning to show signs they could escape human control. In a report released this week, the company said a coordinated global slowdown would "likely be a good thing," but cautioned that if only one company stops, rivals will race ahead. The proposal faces fierce headwinds in Washington, where officials argue any slowdown risks ceding a decisive edge to China. President Trump, however, said he discussed AI safety cooperation with Beijing during his recent visit and signed an executive order requiring 30-day government reviews of the most powerful US models before release. Anthropic also announced plans to set up operations in Singapore.

**Why it matters:** The call from one of the world's top AI labs marks a pivotal moment in the global debate over whether the AI race should slow down — and whether international coordination is even possible.

### #5 | Deadly Delhi Fire Kills at Least 21, Including Foreign Nationals

A fire tore through a multi-storey building operating as an informal bed-and-breakfast in Delhi's Malviya Nagar area, killing at least 21 people and injuring dozens more. Many victims were foreign nationals — from Bangladesh, other South Asian countries, and Africa — who had travelled to India for medical treatment or to accompany relatives receiving care at a nearby private hospital. Eyewitnesses described harrowing scenes: people jumping from upper floors, others found unconscious beneath beds and in washrooms, and neighbours spreading quilts on the ground to cushion those leaping to safety. Prime Minister Narendra Modi announced compensation of 200,000 rupees for families of the deceased. The building's operating permits are now under investigation, with authorities pledging criminal action against those responsible.

**Why it matters:** The tragedy highlights India's persistent building safety crisis and the vulnerability of the country's booming medical tourism industry, which draws patients from across the developing world.
