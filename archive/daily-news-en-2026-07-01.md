## 🌍 Top 5 Global Stories

### #1 | Iran's Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei Dies at 86 — Son Mojtaba Poised to Succeed

Iran's Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei has died at the age of 86, ending a decades-long reign that shaped the Islamic Republic's most consequential years. Funeral preparations are underway in Tehran, with world leaders and regional powers closely watching the transition. His son, Mojtaba Khamenei, widely described as an "unseen leader shadowed by his late father," is expected to succeed him, though the process remains opaque. Khamenei's death marks the end of an era — he was the ruthless revolutionary who held Iran in an iron grip through wars, sanctions, and uprisings.

**Why it matters:** The succession could reshape Iran's nuclear policy, its role in Middle East conflicts, and the fate of the US-Iran tensions still simmering in the Gulf.

🏷️ Geopolitics / Middle East

### #2 | US Supreme Court Upholds Birthright Citizenship, 6-3 Blow to Trump

The US Supreme Court has ruled 6-3 that children born in the United States have a constitutional right to citizenship, rejecting President Trump's executive order to end the 150-year-old policy. Chief Justice John Roberts wrote in the majority opinion that children born to parents "unlawfully or temporarily present" are "citizens at birth" under the 14th Amendment. Trump called the decision "too bad" on Truth Social and vowed to pursue legislation to end birthright citizenship through Congress, calling it "expensive, and unfair to our country."

**Why it matters:** The ruling is the capstone of a Supreme Court term that both dealt Trump heavy defeats and expanded his executive power — a defining tension of his second term.

🏷️ US Politics / Law

### #3 | Venezuela Earthquake: 3-Year-Old Rescued After 6 Days, Death Toll Rises

A three-year-old child has been pulled alive from rubble in Venezuela six days after twin earthquakes devastated coastal regions including La Guaira. The rescue brought a rare moment of hope as the death toll surpassed 1,700 with tens of thousands still missing. Families continue to search desperately through debris — one father told the BBC, "When I turned around, nothing was left." Anger at the government's slow response continues to grow as international aid struggles to reach the hardest-hit areas.

**Why it matters:** One of Latin America's deadliest natural disasters is stretching a fragile state to its breaking point — and testing the international humanitarian system.

🏷️ Disaster / Humanitarian

### #4 | Antwerp Apartment Fire Kills Six in Belgium

A devastating fire ripped through an apartment block in Antwerp, Belgium, killing at least six people. Dramatic footage showed a man clambering through a window to escape thick black smoke from the building, which housed approximately 200 residents. Belgian authorities are investigating the cause of the blaze. The fire is the deadliest residential incident in Belgium in recent years.

**Why it matters:** A tragic reminder of urban fire safety vulnerabilities in aging European housing stock, likely to spark building regulation debates across the EU.

🏷️ Disaster / Europe
