### #1 | Twin 7.2 & 7.5 Magnitude Earthquakes Devastate Venezuela — At Least 32 Dead, 700+ Injured

Two powerful earthquakes struck Venezuela's capital Caracas within 39 seconds of each other on Wednesday evening (local time), killing at least 32 people and injuring around 700, with fears the toll will climb significantly. The first quake, magnitude 7.2, struck Yaracuy state west of Caracas, followed immediately by a stronger 7.5 magnitude tremor at a shallow depth of just 10km — making it one of the strongest temblors to hit the South American nation in a century. Interim President Delcy Rodríguez declared a state of emergency, closing the main international airport, suspending metro and rail services, and pausing school classes for the week. The US Geological Survey warned there is a 36% chance fatalities could reach 10,000 and a 40% chance they could climb as high as 100,000, given the prevalence of vulnerable adobe and unreinforced brick construction in the affected areas. President Trump said Washington was "ready" to assist, while rescuers race through rubble in Caracas and the coastal state of La Guaira to find survivors still heard calling for help.

**Why it matters:** This is the most severe earthquake disaster in Venezuela in a century, striking a densely populated capital region during a national holiday, with international rescue and humanitarian coordination now urgently mobilizing.

### #2 | Trump Requests $87.6 Billion From Congress, Mostly for 'Urgent' Iran War Costs

The White House has formally asked lawmakers to approve $87.6 billion (£66.5bn) in emergency funding, with $67bn earmarked for the Pentagon — including $21bn for munitions, $17.3bn for operational costs, and $12.1bn for classified programmes linked to the ongoing military campaign against Iran. The request also includes $11bn for US farmers and $1.4bn to combat an Ebola outbreak in Central Africa. The proposal faces an uphill battle in Congress: just one day earlier, the Senate passed a bipartisan war powers resolution instructing the president to end the military action, and Trump held a tense, reportedly shouting-fuelled meeting with Senate Republicans over the vote. Although Washington and Tehran are currently observing a ceasefire, the Pentagon argues it needs to "rebuild stocks" after months of strikes. Defence analysts say the conflict has already cost roughly $29bn, and with midterm elections looming in November, the war's unpopularity with voters makes the funding request a political flashpoint.

**Why it matters:** The clash between the White House and Congress over war funding exposes deep rifts in US foreign policy just months before midterm elections, with implications for Middle East stability and global energy markets.

### #3 | Record-Shattering Heatwave Engulfs Western Europe — France Logs Hottest Day Ever

Tens of millions of people across western Europe are enduring a punishing heatwave that has set multiple national records. France recorded its hottest day since records began in 1947, with the national temperature indicator hitting 30°C as daytime highs reached 39–43°C across much of the country. The UK saw its hottest June day on record at 36.1°C in Gosport, Hampshire, with temperatures potentially reaching 38°C on Thursday. Spain logged its highest daily average June temperatures since 1950 on both Monday and Tuesday. Red heat alerts have been issued across swathes of France, the UK, Italy, and Spain. The Louvre museum and Eiffel Tower in Paris closed early because the buildings are "not sufficiently adapted to climate change," while wildfires erupted in western France and drowning deaths were reported in Germany as people sought relief in rivers. The heatwave is expected to spread eastward to Germany, Poland, Croatia, and Hungary by the weekend.

**Why it matters:** Europe is the world's fastest-warming continent, heating at twice the global average, and this record-breaking event underscores the accelerating real-world impact of climate change on infrastructure, public health, and daily life.

🏷️ Climate / Europe

### #4 | Elon Musk Loses Trillionaire Status as Global Tech Rout Hammers SpaceX

Elon Musk's net worth fell back below the trillion-dollar mark to $957 billion on Tuesday, less than two weeks after he became the first person in history to cross that threshold following SpaceX's blockbuster Nasdaq IPO. The reversal came as a broad technology sell-off, fuelled by investor anxiety over AI capital spending, stubborn interest rates, and doubts about long-term profitability, sent SpaceX shares plunging more than 30% from their mid-June peak of $225.64 to around $156. On a single turbulent Monday, a 16% drop erased an estimated $240 billion from Musk's personal balance sheet; Tesla shares slid nearly 6% the very next day. Musk's fortune is uniquely concentrated — SpaceX represents nearly 80% of his net worth — making him exceptionally vulnerable to post-IPO volatility. Analysts note that insider share lockups expire in late July, potentially adding further pressure, though a modest 6% recovery in SpaceX stock would restore his 13-figure status.

**Why it matters:** The dramatic rise and partial fall of the world's first trillionaire reflects broader market anxiety about whether the AI and space-tech investment boom is built on solid fundamentals or speculative hype.

🏷️ Global Economy / Technology

### #5 | Anthropic Accuses Alibaba of 'Brazen' AI Theft — 29 Million Fraudulent Exchanges

US artificial intelligence company Anthropic has accused Chinese tech giant Alibaba of carrying out "the largest campaign to illicitly extract Claude's capabilities" ever recorded, in a letter sent to US Senators Tim Scott and Elizabeth Warren. The San Francisco-based firm alleges that operators linked to the New York Stock Exchange-listed Alibaba used thousands of fraudulent accounts to conduct nearly 29 million exchanges with Anthropic's Claude AI model through so-called "distillation attacks" — a technique that extracts answers from a stronger AI model to train a weaker one. Anthropic warned that such operations are conducted on an "industrial scale" to enable Chinese companies to "harvest and repackage US AI capabilities as their own," effectively turning hundreds of billions of dollars in American R&D investment into a subsidy for geopolitical competitors. The accusation escalates an already tense US-China technology rivalry, following similar allegations made previously by OpenAI against Chinese groups. Alibaba has not yet publicly responded to the claims.

**Why it matters:** This allegation marks a new frontier in US-China tech competition, framing AI model security as a matter of national and economic security that could shape future export controls and regulation.
