## 🌍 Top 5 Global Stories

### #1 | US and Iran 'Very Close' to Ceasefire Extension Deal, VP Vance Says

A framework for extending the US-Iran ceasefire by 60 days and launching talks on Iran's nuclear programme has reportedly been agreed, pending approval from President Trump and Iran's leadership. Vice President JD Vance told the BBC the two sides were "very close" but still working through "a couple of language points" including the question of uranium enrichment. The tentative deal would allow unrestricted passage through the Strait of Hormuz, with Iran given 30 days to remove mines, while the US would lift its naval blockade and issue sanctions waivers for Iranian oil sales. However, both countries continue to accuse each other of violating the fragile ceasefire that began on April 8, with Iran's IRGC claiming it targeted a US base on Thursday and fresh US strikes reported in southern Iran.

**Why it matters:** A deal would restore the flow of one-fifth of the world's oil and LNG through the Strait of Hormuz, while opening the door to resolving the Iran nuclear standoff — but the gap between diplomatic optimism and battlefield reality remains dangerously wide.

### #2 | Netanyahu Orders IDF to Expand Gaza Control to 70%, Defying Ceasefire Terms

Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu announced he has directed the IDF to increase control of Gaza from the current 60% to 70%, directly contradicting the terms of the US-brokered ceasefire agreed with Hamas in October 2025. "We're pressing them from all sides, we'll deal with the remnants," Netanyahu said at a conference on Thursday. The expansion comes as indirect talks between Israel and Hamas remain stalled, and at least 738 Palestinians have been killed since the ceasefire came into effect, according to the Hamas-run health ministry. The total death toll in Gaza now stands at over 72,700, including more than 21,000 children, per UN-acknowledged figures.

**Why it matters:** The unilateral expansion signals that the ceasefire is collapsing and raises the prospect of resumed full-scale conflict, further destabilising the Middle East and undermining US diplomatic credibility in the region.

### #3 | Blue Origin New Glenn Rocket Explodes During Test at Cape Canaveral

A Blue Origin New Glenn rocket burst into a massive fireball during a hotfire test at Cape Canaveral, Florida, on Thursday night. The company, founded by Jeff Bezos, confirmed all personnel were accounted for with no injuries, and described the incident as an "anomaly." NASA Administrator Jared Isaacman acknowledged the difficulty of developing heavy-lift launch capability, pledging a thorough investigation. This marks the second major setback for Blue Origin in two months, following April's failed satellite launch that prompted an FAA investigation. Bezos said it was a "very rough day" but vowed to rebuild and return to flight.

**Why it matters:** The explosion deals a significant blow to the commercial space industry's heavy-lift ambitions and could delay NASA's launch manifest, which increasingly relies on private-sector rockets from both Blue Origin and SpaceX.

### #4 | Europe Swelters Under Record-Breaking Heatwave; Portugal Hits 40.3°C

Western Europe is in the grip of an extreme May heatwave, with Portugal shattering its hottest May day on record at 40.3°C in the town of Mora — exceeding the previous 2001 record of 40°C. Italy issued red heatwave alerts for Rome, Florence, and four other cities warning of health risks for healthy and active people. France placed 17 departments under orange alert, with schools closing as indoor temperatures hit 53°C in some buildings. The heatwave, caused by a "heat dome" high-pressure system, is forecast to continue through the weekend. The UN warned on Thursday that global average temperatures are likely to stay at or near record levels for the next four years.

**Why it matters:** This off-the-charts May heat is a stark preview of climate-accelerated summer extremes that threaten public health, agriculture, and infrastructure across the continent — and a warning to the rest of the Northern Hemisphere.

### #5 | Japan and Philippines Deepen Defense Alliance with Arms Sales on the Table

Japanese Prime Minister Sanae Takaichi hosted Philippine President Ferdinand Marcos Jr. for a state visit in Tokyo on Thursday, with the two leaders agreeing to strengthen defense ties and discussing arms sales aimed at deterring China's growing military assertiveness in the South China Sea. Marcos addressed Japan's parliament during the visit, which included a welcome ceremony with Emperor Naruhito at the Imperial Palace. The deepening Tokyo-Manila axis reflects Japan's expanding security role in Southeast Asia as both nations face escalating maritime tensions with Beijing over disputed waters and Taiwan contingency planning.

**Why it matters:** The Japan-Philippines defense partnership is rapidly becoming a cornerstone of the Indo-Pacific security architecture, signalling to Beijing that its maritime expansion faces an increasingly coordinated coalition of democratic allies.
