### #1 | US-Iran Military Escalation: Oil Tankers Struck Near Oman as Tensions Boil Over

The United States confirmed strikes on two vessels — the Settebello and Marivex — near Oman, with the attack killing three Indian sailors aboard the second tanker. A third vessel, the Jalveer, came under suspected US fire but all 20 crew members were reported safe. The strikes come amid rapidly deteriorating US-Iran relations, with hopes of a quick peace deal fading. India coordinated rescue efforts through its Navy and Embassy, while Oman rescued 24 Indian seafarers from the Marivex. This marks one of the most serious direct military confrontations between the two nations in the ongoing conflict.

**Why it matters:** The escalation in the Strait of Hormuz and Gulf of Oman threatens global oil shipping lanes and could trigger a wider regional war, with direct consequences for energy prices worldwide.

### #2 | Trump Says "I Love the Inflation" as US Prices Hit Three-Year High

US consumer prices rose at the fastest rate in three years, putting the Trump administration's tariff-driven economic policy under intense scrutiny. President Trump later clarified he meant he was "glad inflation was not higher," but the remark sparked widespread criticism. The second-term administration's tariffs on much of the world, aimed at re-industrializing America, have been blamed by experts for worsening inflation rather than fighting it. The economic climate is shaping up as a central issue ahead of the November 2026 midterm elections.

**Why it matters:** Inflation and tariff policy are defining the 2026 political landscape, with voters heading to the polls in November to decide control of Congress.

### #3 | US 2026 Midterm Primaries Underway: Control of Congress on the Line

Primary elections are being held across multiple states as the nation gears up for the November 3 midterms, where all 435 House seats and 35 Senate seats are up for grabs. The elections will determine control of the 120th Congress during Donald Trump's second term. Key races include gubernatorial contests in 36 states and three territories. The backdrop includes debates over tariffs, immigration enforcement under the One Big Beautiful Bill Act, and the ongoing inflation crisis.

**Why it matters:** These midterms will test the strength of Trump's political coalition and could fundamentally shift the balance of power in Washington.

### #4 | Xi Jinping Returns from North Korea: Ceremony at Korean War Monument Marks Historic Visit

Chinese President Xi Jinping and First Lady Peng Liyuan held a ceremony at the Korean War monument in Pyongyang on June 10, evoking what state media called "eternal historical memory." Xi's multi-day visit to North Korea — his first in years — featured meetings with Kim Jong-un, a thank-you call after departure, and coordinated messaging about a "new chapter" in bilateral ties. Analysts interpreted the visit as a pivot toward trade cooperation over denuclearization demands. The trip comes during China's APEC 2026 hosting year, with the Second Senior Officials' Meeting recently held in Shanghai in May.

**Why it matters:** Xi's North Korea visit signals a strategic realignment in Northeast Asia, strengthening the China-North Korea axis at a time of heightened US tensions in the region.

### #5 | Applied Materials Opens $500M Singapore Campus as AI Demand Drives Semiconductor Boom

Applied Materials officially opened a US$500 million manufacturing campus in Singapore, part of a strategy to nearly double its global production capacity. The expansion is driven by surging AI demand for advanced semiconductors. In parallel, SK hynix announced plans to triple wafer output by 2034 and reach 375-layer NAND by year-end. Meta also partnered with Reliance Industries to develop a 168 MW AI-enabled data centre in Gujarat, India. Singapore's role as a semiconductor hub continues to grow, with the new campus reinforcing its position in the global chip supply chain.

**Why it matters:** The AI-driven semiconductor buildout is reshaping global supply chains, with Singapore emerging as a critical manufacturing node between the US, China, and the rest of Asia.
